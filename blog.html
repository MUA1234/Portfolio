    Worked as a volunteer for FOSS(Free & Open Source Software)

    Joining the FOSS (Free & Open Source Software) club was one of the most enriching experiences of my academic journey. From the very first meeting, I was captivated by the club's mission to promote open-source principles and foster a community where knowledge and resources are shared freely. Volunteering for FOSS was not just about contributing to projects; it was about becoming part of a movement that believes in the power of collaboration and transparency. One of the highlights of my time in the FOSS club was participating in various hackathons. These events were intense, but they were also incredibly rewarding. Working alongside passionate peers, we brainstormed innovative solutions, wrote code late into the night, and often surprised ourselves with what we could achieve in such a short time. Each hackathon was a unique challenge that pushed our boundaries and honed our skills. The thrill of presenting our projects and receiving feedback from industry professionals was an invaluable learning experience. In addition to hackathons, I also volunteered for the club by contributing code to various open-source projects. This was a fantastic way to apply what I had learned in my courses and gain real-world experience. Collaborating with developers from around the world, I improved my coding skills and learned the importance of writing clean, efficient, and well-documented code. Each contribution, no matter how small, felt significant because it was part of a larger effort to create tools and resources that anyone could use and improve upon. Moreover, I had the opportunity to engage in activities like signing and outreach, which helped raise awareness about the importance of open-source software. These activities were crucial in building a vibrant and inclusive community. By educating others about the benefits of open-source and encouraging them to contribute, we aimed to create a ripple effect that would extend beyond our club. The sense of camaraderie and shared purpose within FOSS was truly inspiring, and it taught me the value of working together towards a common goal.

    Worked as a CSSL member, volunteer, and a student

    Being a member of the Computer Society of Sri Lanka (CSSL) has been an enriching experience, blending professional growth with community service. As a volunteer, I’ve had the privilege of contributing to various initiatives, fostering a deeper connection with the tech community. The camaraderie among members and the shared passion for technology create a vibrant atmosphere, making every project and event a rewarding journey. My journey with CSSL also included a transformative four-month UI/UX designing coursework. This intensive program was meticulously structured to provide both theoretical knowledge and practical skills. From understanding the fundamentals of user interface design to mastering the intricacies of user experience, the coursework was a deep dive into the world of design. Each module was crafted to build on the previous one, ensuring a comprehensive understanding of the subject. Throughout the coursework, I had the opportunity to work on real-world projects, applying the principles learned in class to create intuitive and aesthetically pleasing designs. The hands-on experience was invaluable, allowing me to experiment with different design techniques and receive constructive feedback from experienced mentors. This practical approach not only honed my skills but also boosted my confidence in tackling complex design challenges. Being a part of CSSL has been a cornerstone of my professional journey. It has provided me with a platform to learn, grow, and give back to the community. The UI/UX coursework, in particular, has been a highlight, equipping me with the skills and knowledge to excel in the dynamic field of design. As I continue to explore the ever-evolving landscape of technology, I carry with me the lessons and experiences gained from my time with CSSL.
